KEN THOMPSON

About Himself

Ken Thompson was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, on February 4th, 1943. He got his master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the University of California, Berkeley. He is the creator of UNIX operating system, B programming language which begeted C programming language. [1] [2]

His Computer-Career

Thompson and his friend ,Dennis Ritchie, studied in Bell Laboratories in 1969 [2] ( Image 1) (Image 2). In that time, they did their biggest increase, designed UNIX operating systems. Firstly,Thompson has encoded the first version of UNIX in a short time. It was too new and there were a lot of work to do to run UNIX. In the end of that time, there were over 20 UNIX systems. [3]

He made UTF8 (Unicode Transformation Format) with Rob Pike and encoded the best chess game in that time. In 1983, he and his friend Ritch get Alan Turing Award because of their labours. After he retired from Bell Laboratories, he began to work on Google's programming language Go. As known, he is still working on 'Go'. [3]
